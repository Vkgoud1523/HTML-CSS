Validating Passengers Identity

The widespread adoption of e-Passports in particular has reduced the opportunity for identity fraud.

Biometric data, including face and fingerprints, is digitally encoded within a chip embedded in the physical ePassport book enabling live biometric checks of the holder, ensuring that the person carrying the passport is the owner.

These biometric checks can be used at every point of the journey . Once enrolled, after the first touch point in the journey through the airport, there is no need for the passenger to present a boarding pass, a passport or travel documents again. 

Combination of Iris + Surname fields >> hashed >> Published on blockchain.

Tracking Missing Luggage

The airlines lose millions of bags each year costing them hundreds of millions of dollars each year

Airlines must adopt smart technologies through, IoT, Cloud and blockchain to reduce baggage mishandling

Radio Frequency Identification (RFID).  Blockchain helps overcome the above problems faced by both entities. The RFID tags is used to sense the location of the luggage and track them at checkpoints and simultaneously update the blockchain.

Blockchain Technology can be translated as a timestamped record and hence can be used to trace the location of the bag at a particular point in time after it is added to the blockchain

Public Sector

The management of public services is yet another area where blockchain can help lessen paper-based processes, minimize fraud, and increase accountability between authorities and those they serve.

While Organizations are only accountable to their shareholders, Governments are accountable to a country and its people.

The technologies can be leveraged in government and the public sector to streamline operations and enhance transparency and accountability. Use cases range from Self-Sovereign digital identity, to Electronic health records and Central banking.

Voting

The e-voting system we use now is susceptible to hacks or meddling from the organizations that create the infrastructure.

If the voting database will exist on the blockchain that millions of nodes will support simultaneously. This ensures that the data cannot be changed or manipulated. Also, due to the robust encryption and decentralization, the voting database will be incorruptible, and each voting record will be easily verifiable.

Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)

A central bank digital currency is the digital form of a country's fiat currency.

A CBDC is issued and regulated by a nation's monetary authority or central bank.

CBDCs promote financial inclusion and simplify the implementation of monetary and fiscal policy.

As a centralized form of currency, they may not anonymize transactions as some cryptocurrencies do.

Many countries are exploring how CBDCs will affect their economies, existing financial networks, and stability.

Blockchain gives students ownership of their personal records, allowing them to control their academic identity.

Academic blockchain is a completely distributed and decentralized system that requires all participating peers to install node applications which facilitates the node to access the student's records in the blockchain

Blockchain can allow educators to publish content openly as well as tracking any re-use, without putting limitations on the source material. With Blockchain, the agreements of automatic transfer of credits can be written as smart-contracts.

Libraries could deploy a blockchain-based system layered with “smart contract” code to facilitate the indexing and sharing of community items (tools, cars, expertise) in a sharing network.

A blockchain solution can allow telecom service providers to store unique device/SIM data on the blockchain along with customer profile, block a stolen device instantaneously and also keep third parties informed of any change of status of a device.
